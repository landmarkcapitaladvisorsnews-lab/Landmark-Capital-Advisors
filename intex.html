Future of Real Estate: Ashish Joshi on India’s Structural Growth Shift

Mumbai (Maharashtra) [India], February XX: India’s real estate sector is no longer defined merely by expansion or cyclical recovery. It is entering a structural growth phase shaped by institutional capital, governance discipline, and long-term economic momentum. According to Ashish Joshi, Founder of Landmark Capital Advisors, the future of Indian real estate will be determined less by speculative appreciation and more by structural fundamentals.

As India strengthens its position among the world’s fastest-growing major economies, capital flows into property markets are becoming deeper, more strategic, and increasingly institutional. In this evolving environment, Ashish Joshi believes the next decade will reward precision, execution capability, and governance-led investing.

A Structural Shift, Not a Cyclical Rally

For years, real estate cycles in India were closely tied to liquidity waves and macro sentiment. Growth phases were often accompanied by aggressive price expansion, while downturns exposed over-leveraged structures.

However, the current phase appears different.

“This is not just another upcycle,” Ashish Joshi explains. “We are witnessing a structural strengthening of the ecosystem from regulatory transparency to institutional participation.”

Landmark Capital Advisors has observed that investor conversations have evolved significantly. Where earlier discussions revolved around short-term IRR targets, today’s allocators are more focused on income durability, downside protection, and asset-level clarity.

This shift reflects broader economic stability and regulatory maturity.

Institutional Capital Is Reshaping the Landscape

One of the defining features of India’s structural growth shift is the rising participation of institutional capital both domestic and global.

Institutional inflows into Indian real estate have remained robust in recent years, spanning office, logistics, data centers, and premium residential assets. But beyond the quantum of capital, its character has changed.

According to Ashish Joshi, capital entering today’s market is longer-term in nature. “Institutions are prioritizing governance, reporting clarity, and sustainable yield profiles,” he notes.

Landmark Capital has seen increasing preference for structured vehicles, asset-level transparency, and disciplined capital recycling frameworks. This marks a transition from momentum-driven investing to process-driven allocation.

Office Real Estate: Quality Over Quantity

India continues to attract multinational corporations and Global Capability Centers (GCCs), reinforcing demand for Grade-A office space across major cities.

However, the growth is increasingly selective.

Prime assets with strong connectivity, sustainability standards, and institutional management are outperforming older inventory. Rental resilience in high-quality assets contrasts sharply with pressure on secondary stock.

Ashish Joshi emphasizes that future returns will depend on tenant strength and operational execution rather than broad market optimism.

“Quality-led dispersion will define performance,” he says. “Investors must differentiate between structural demand and cyclical exuberance.”

Residential: From Momentum to Stability

Residential markets have delivered strong performance in select micro-markets over the past two years. Yet, the structural growth shift implies normalization rather than overheating.

Urban housing demand is increasingly end-user driven, supported by income growth, urbanization, and demographic strength.

Landmark Capital Advisors believes that disciplined underwriting is critical in this environment. Execution timelines, capital structure, and developer governance will influence realized returns more than headline launch pricing.

Ashish Joshi notes that investors must evaluate risk holistically, particularly in under-construction and premium segments where capital deployment spans multiple years.

Governance as the Growth Multiplier

A defining aspect of India’s structural shift is regulatory evolution. Enhanced transparency in alternative investment frameworks and stricter compliance standards have strengthened investor confidence.

According to Ashish Joshi, governance is no longer a compliance exercise — it is a competitive advantage.

“At Landmark Capital Advisors, governance is embedded in structuring philosophy,” he says. “Long-term capital aligns with platforms that prioritize clarity and alignment.”

Institutional allocators increasingly assess not just projected returns, but reporting integrity, risk frameworks, and incentive alignment.

This governance-driven approach is shaping a more stable investment environment.

The Rise of Income-Oriented Strategies

Historically, a significant portion of returns in Indian real estate came from valuation expansion. However, cap-rate compression in prime assets has moderated.

Going forward, income visibility will anchor performance.

Ashish Joshi explains that rental growth, leasing discipline, and net operating income expansion will become primary return drivers. Structured asset management and refinancing strategy will separate outperformers from passive participants.

Landmark Capital has observed growing investor interest in income-backed strategies, particularly in stabilized commercial and hybrid asset classes.

This reflects confidence in India’s structural growth trajectory while acknowledging the importance of disciplined execution.

Operational Alpha Will Define the Next Decade

As markets mature, operational capability becomes a critical differentiator.

Leasing expertise, asset repositioning, sustainability integration, and financial structuring are no longer secondary considerations they are central to value creation.

Ashish Joshi believes that the future of real estate will reward platforms capable of generating operational alpha rather than relying solely on macro tailwinds.

“India’s growth story remains compelling,” he says. “But the composition of returns is evolving.”

At Landmark Capital Advisors, structured deployment and phased capital strategies are designed to reduce volatility and enhance long-term credibility.

Looking Ahead: Precision Over Expansion

India’s structural growth shift does not signal a slowdown. Instead, it reflects maturation.

Urbanization, infrastructure expansion, demographic momentum, and economic resilience continue to support real estate demand. However, investors must adapt to a more selective environment.

Ashish Joshi concludes that the next phase will reward:

  • Governance integrity

  • Asset-level precision

  • Income durability

  • Operational execution

“The future of Indian real estate belongs to disciplined capital,” he says.

As India advances through this structural transformation, the sector appears less driven by speculative cycles and more by institutional depth. In this evolving landscape, strategic clarity and execution excellence may ultimately determine long-term success.
